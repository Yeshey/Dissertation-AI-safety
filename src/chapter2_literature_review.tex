\chapter{Literature Review 1 - AI Safety}\label{chap:literature_review}

This SLR will target the continuous research in the area of AI safety by narrowing on a specified search string capable of funneling the amount of peer reviewed articles from several known Datasets.

\section{AI safety and research}

By picking relevant keywords we constructed a search string designed to narrow the amount of relevant articles in the target Databases.

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Search String:} ("artificial intelligence" OR "AI" OR "generative AI" OR "gen AI" OR "genAI" ) AND
("LLM*" OR "large language model*" OR "multimodal large language model*" OR "MLLM" OR "GAI" OR "AGI" OR "Artificial General Intelligence") AND
("subversion" OR "bypass" OR "jailbreak*" OR "guardrail*" OR "adversarial attack*" OR "reward hacking" OR "trusted model*" OR "untrusted model*" OR "treacherous turn" OR "specification gaming" OR
"AI control" OR "AI alignment" OR "AI misalignment" OR "value alignment" OR "mesa-optimization" OR "power-seeking" OR "deceptive alignment" OR "orthogonality thesis" OR "instrumental convergence" OR "outer alignment" OR "inner alignment" OR "AI takeoff" OR "emergent goals" OR "sleeper agent*" OR "alignment faking" OR "windfall clause") AND NOT ( "robot*" OR "industrial" OR "manufacturing" OR "user experience" OR "UX" OR "clinical" OR "healthcare" OR "medical")
    \item \textbf{Datasets:} The search engines used were two brokers: Scopus\footnote{1https://www.scopus.com} and Web of Science\footnote{https://apps.webofknowledge.com}, in conjunction with IEEE\footnote{https://ieeexplore.ieee.org}, ACM\footnote{https://dl.acm.org}, EBSCO\footnote{https://search.ebscohost.com}, Taylor \& Francis\footnote{https://taylorandfrancis.com/} and Jstor\footnote{https://www.jstor.org/}.
\end{itemize}
