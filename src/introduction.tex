	This chapter is meant to contextualize the work explored and tackled throughout the dissertation by precisely defining the problem statement, the intended objectives, and the initial motivation for picking this area.

	\section{Motivation}

	To steer the research objectives it is helpful to determine the exact motivation behind the work. Up until now, every technological achievement merely represented an extension of our capabilities. Now, with \gls{ai}, for the first time there is the prospect of a technology that would have its own goals and be capable of substituting the inventive process itself, rendering human capabilities obsolete \cite{bostrom2014superintelligence, salmon_managing_2023}. The fact that an advanced \gls{ai} could autonomously pursue its own objectives poses unprecedented risks to humanity, as these internally generated goals may fundamentally conflict with human values and evade our capacity for control \cite{yampolskiy_controllability_2022, omohundro2008basic}. It is the urgent need to address these vulnerabilities and ensure that future \gls{ai} systems remain safely aligned with human survival and well-being that drives this research.

	\section{Problem}

	The dangers of unaligned \gls{ai} frequently start with Bostrom’s “Paperclip Maximizer” \cite{bostrom2014superintelligence}, which demonstrates how an \gls{ai} system with seemingly benign goals could still potentially lead to catastrophic outcomes if not properly aligned with human values. This thought experiment is rooted in the orthogonality thesis, which states that there is no inherent link between a system's goals and its ability to reach them.
	Building on these concerns, Yampolskiy argues that it's definitionally impossible for humans to understand or control a super-intelligent agent \cite{yampolskiy_controllability_2022}. Such arguments have motivated calls for caution in the development of advanced \gls{ai} systems, including public statements advocating for the suspension or regulation of certain forms of \gls{ai} research until adequate safety guarantees can be established \cite{FLI_SuperintelligenceStatement2025}.

	Today, Multi-modal and Generalist \glspl{llm} are capable of performing tasks in a large range of domains, exhibiting early signs of instrumental convergence behavior \cite{greenblatt_alignment_faking}.

	Furthermore, current societal structure mandates, through competition and rivalry, that there will be large amounts of money invested in trying to reach such capable systems, and there are rising concerns that \gls{ai} companies have too great financial incentives to avoid effective self-regulating oversight \cite{hemphill_advanced_2025}. This is corroborated by several \gls{ai} safety reports that reveal how lacking the approach of current \gls{ai} companies is towards \gls{ai} safety \cite{FLIAISafetyIndex2025, AISafetyReport2025}.

	\section{Objectives}

	This research aims to find out if the current pursuit of \gls{agi} has natural dangers, how dangerous they are, and whether they are relevant in general or only in narrow domains. To achieve this, the work will be conducted initially through a \gls{slr}, which will later inform and guide practical experiments.

	Thus, the literature review shall focus on work that has been carried out in analyzing the intent and inner objectives of current generalist and narrow models. For that purpose, the following research questions will be used to guide the \gls{slr}:

	\begin{enumerate}
		\item \gls{rq}1. What is the main focus of current \gls{ai} safety research?
		\item \gls{rq}2. Which are the current methods used to look into the alignment of current generalist models?
		\item \gls{rq}3. What conclusions regarding the safety of current models and safety of development and deployment processes can we assert from current research?
		\item \gls{rq}4. Is there a measurable gap between the research attention given to an AI safety area and its assessed level of danger?
	\end{enumerate}

	After conclusions are drawn from these research questions, a path for the most relevant \gls{ai} safety research field to explore via practical experiments can be laid out.
